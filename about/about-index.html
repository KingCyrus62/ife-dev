01 // My Story

Computer Engineering student, music producer-ish, programmer based in Nigeria.

01 // MY STORY

I've always been the curious cat...

I've always been the kind of person who asks questions. If something catches my attention, I want to understand how it works, why it works, and whether I can build something like it myself. That curiosity has shaped a lot of the decisions -and put me in a lot of trouble growing up- but that curiosity continues to influence the way I approach learning every day.

A small backstory to how I even got interested in computers: It all started back when I was like 6 years old and my grandpa had one and I was so intrigued because he started teaching my how to type on one of the earliest versions of MS Word and it was cool even though I didn't know to use the space bar to put space between my words but I enjoyed it. Today, I'm studying Computer Engineering in the University of Ilorin, a field that perfectly combines my interest in technology, problem-solving, and creativity, If you know me in real life, I don't like school but I like computers so I might as well just study what I love, right?
What excites me most isn't just learning how computers work—it's the idea of using that knowledge to create things that can make a difference, no matter how big or small.

But my journey isn't defined by engineering alone. I'm someone who enjoys exploring different interests and constantly challenging myself. Whether I'm learning a new programming language, experimenting with music production in FL Studio, improving my basketball game, or doing some calisthenics, -I even considered content creation this year so don't be surprised if u see me on twitter with a hundred million subscribers- I genuinely enjoy the process of starting as a beginner and gradually improving through consistency. I hate the fact that its hard and tiring sometimes and sometimes I genuinely just give up, slack off, sleep and quit and that has made me drop alot of stuff I started working on but overall I enjoy that initial thrill of learning something new and the satisfaction that comes with seeing progress over time.

One very important lessons I learnt this past year is that its okay to take a break and recharge. It's easy to get caught up in the hustle of learning and achieving, but taking time for yourself is just as important. I've learned that rest isn't laziness—it's a necessary part of the growth process. Some of my biggest lessons haven't come from learning or coding or gaming. They've come from people. From conversations, friendships, disappointments, and moments that forced me to grow. Those experiences have taught me resilience, perspective, and the importance of continuing to move forward even when life hurts too much to continue.

This website is a reflection of that journey. It's a place where I can share the projects I'm building, document the lessons I'm learning, and look back on how far I've come. I'm still writing the first chapters of my story, and that's what makes the future so exciting.

02 // Beyond Engineering

Showing the human behind the degree.

While code and engineering take up a massive chunk of my brain layout, they don't tell the whole story. I’ve never believed in being just a student wrapped up entirely in textbooks or screen glare. For me, keeping a high-performance output in my work requires stepping completely away from the desk to touch grass and stay active.

That relief happens on the basketball court. Basketball isn't just a casual hobby; it’s where I go to completely clear my head, practice split-second strategy, and work on my handles. The same discipline it takes to execute a clean play or push through a hard physical workout maps directly onto debugging a complex program or staying patient when a project layout breaks.

Navigating university life has taught me that the best growth happens through a mix of diverse experiences. Interacting with people outside the classroom, learning from different perspectives, and balancing an aggressive side-hustle alongside my academic workload is what keeps me sharp, grounded, and human.
